About Me

I am a musician and technologist. My passion is producing new sounds and tools for music creation by combining experimentation with informed, practical knowledge. My expertise includes music production, computer programming for sound design and synthesis applications, and computer-based sound installations and performance. I am also highly capable and experienced working with data; I have a degree in mathematics and previously worked for 4+ years as a paraprofessional accountant and systems support technician.

Prior to moving to the Bay Area, I lived in Valencia, Spain where I earned a master’s degree in music technology and production from Berklee College of Music. I graduated summa cum laude thanks to my work ethic and dedication to the subject. My classmates selected me as the recipient of the MTI Peer Recognition Award because of my willingness to support and assist them with their work. I love to help others find their creative outlet by sharing my knowledge and experience.

As an undergraduate at the University of Virginia I studied both music and mathematics. I prospered as part of the McIntire Department of Music, receiving the Brander Wyatt Morrison Prize and an award for Excellence in Music Research and Ethnography upon graduation. I also began studying computer science, knowledge that I continue to build on today.

I approach each step of my career optimistically, maintaining a common thread of avid learning. I am currently working diligently to improve at C, C++, and Ruby on Rails to build audio plug-ins and web applications. My goal for my next career step is to find a position in which I can combine all of my various talents and utilize them as an audio programmer, audio engineer, product designer, or product manager.

If you feel that I can contribute to your organization, please contact me through my LinkedIn profile or at timothyshull@gmail.com.
