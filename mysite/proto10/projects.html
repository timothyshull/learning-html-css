Beginning in late 2014, I began learning and relearning a number of software and development platforms through self-guided practice. During this time I have studied and experimented with web development, iOS development, Cocoa/OSX development, the specifics of languages such as C++ and JavaScript, and computer science essentials such as data structures and design patterns. Additionally, I have also devoted time to gaining a better understanding of the architecture and inner workings of computers.

Check out the folders labeled "learning" on my GitHub page to see some of these projects. Most of this work was done in the name of figuring out some small concept or solving a simple problem while also speeding up my workflow. It is for this reason and because these were intended more as exercises than portfolio pieces that some of the projects are relatively empty.

Additionally, I just started working with a friend on a project to create a node based MVVM framework for quickly generating todo lists and forms with Kendo UI components. We have mainly just brainstormed and Mike has layed the groundwork…mikep175/map

Max/MSP/Jitter & Max4Live

Working with Max is very fulfilling because the visual environment is extremely accessible but it still reveals a lot of the inner workings of digital audio and video. I have chosen the patches here out of the many that I have created because they were very useful for me as sound design tools. CustomWaveFuzz allows you to draw in a waveform that is used for waveshaping distortion and saturation and it also contains tone and filter knobs modeled after a famous brand of production amplifier.

BufferGranulator was a patch that I designed to visually demonstrate granular synthesis techniques while mirroring them sonically.

Arduino & Sound Design

For my first Arduino project, I hacked together the sound makers from a few children's toys, wired control of them into the Arduino, and created a microphone loop that could be controlled by Maxuino to resample and further manipulate the sounds.

Some of the interactive elements of Timelines will be built using the Arduino.

Other Projects

The Rule of Three

The Rule of Three initially formed for a project in Spain, but we continued to work together afterwards. The idea was to compose and perform improvisationally using only the Orbit and our laptops. We encountered a few difficulties imposed by time and technology for the EmTech performance, but it was fun to work with these guys.

Bandazian

This relic of the internet is one of the few remaining testaments to a band that I devoted a good portion of my life to. We made so many recordings that are now lost and played a lot of dingy house shows together and this band will always mean a lot to me.

But alas, we broke-up.

Cobra Horse

Cobra Horse was a really fun band to play with because those guys were and are some kick-ass rock musicians. They were lucky enough to open for Band of Horses and Archers of Loaf right after I left the band to move away from Asheville.

Brian McGee

Brian McGee is an amazing folk/country songwriter and I really enjoyed playing upright bass with him for a little while.

Matt Rumley

I lived and played music with Matt Rumley during a period of intense musical growth. Listening to Matt and his brother Kevin practice music when we were young is what inspired me to start playing music and I have learned so much from both of them.

And you should definitely support Matt and Kevin's current project Her Marigold!

Angi West

I am also incredibly lucky to have performed with a songwriter as talented as Angi. She is a masterful vocalist and her wisdom and caring show through in her music. An anecdote that I will probably mention if you ever meet me is that we used to practice in an apartment in the upstairs of Bob Moog’s house…somewhat ironically nothing was ever electrified.

At the very least this group deserves an honorable mention. We recorded an EP that was never "officially" released, but it contained 4 songs filled with gritty, smokey, North Carolina mountain folk goodness. This guy was among the ranks.

Wages - Timelines

Timelines is a, "conceptual installation and performance illustrating humanity's unique place within the framework of time - living finite lives within the seemingly infinite. The piece will utilize cascading sand (a symbol of time) to play instruments, creating a haunting, beautiful sound that is as visually unique as it is musical and conceptual." For this project I am creating a number of interactive elements using Arduino and other DIY electronics that will allow audience interaction with the both the live performances and the installation. These elements will be tied into an application that will process the sound and in ways that correlate to the experience of the audience's interactions and will also be able to be played as an intstrument in it's own right.

3D Sound & Synthesis

3D Sound & Synthesis was a sound installation that I created and displayed while in Valencia. For this installation I started by creating a three-dimensional sound panner of my own design; combining an OpenGL graphical interface, the accelerometers in the Numark Orbit, and the computer's own keyboard to facilitate a variety of potential interactions. For the sonic environment, I synthesized randomly generated water droplets created using a filtered impulse that were then spatialized using convolution reverb and the three-dimensional logic from the panner. The final element was the sound of a fly, synthesized using a filtered sawtooth wave that was modeled to account for the Doppler effect and spatially controlled using the Orbit.

John Cage's Four6

This image is taken from a performance of John Cage's piece Four6 with Ben Houge, Michael Sean Harris, and Alek Palmersmith at the Museu de Belles Arts in València, Spain. For this performance, we used an iOS app to dictate the score and I used various guitar preparations to create interesting sonic events.
